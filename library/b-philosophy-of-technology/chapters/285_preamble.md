Medical ethics is that branch of applied ethics that is concerned with the ethical problems of healthcare professionals and healthcare systems. It is a subset of bioethics, and can itself be further divided in medical ethics (narrowly defined), nursing ethics, public health ethics, research ethics, management ethics, etc.

There are extensive overlaps between the field of medical ethics and cognate fields such as the ethics of genetics (genethics), the ethics of new technologies, and professional ethics in general.
